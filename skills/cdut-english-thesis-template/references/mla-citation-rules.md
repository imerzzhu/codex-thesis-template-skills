# CDUT English Thesis MLA And Citation Rules

The supplied reference template states that Works Cited and parenthetical citations follow MLA Handbook for Writers of Research Papers, seventh edition, with local Chinese-source conventions.

## Works Cited Heading

- Use `Works Cited`, plural.
- Do not write `Work Cited`.
- Start on a new page.
- Do not add a chapter number or punctuation after the heading.
- Align left; use small 2, bold; add about 0.5 line spacing before.

## Ordering

- Put English-language references before Chinese-language references.
- Sort English entries alphabetically by author surname.
- Sort Chinese entries after English entries.
- For multiple works by the same author, use MLA's repeated-author dash convention in English entries: `---.`.

## English Reference Patterns

- Book by one author: author surname first, title italicized, publisher, year, medium.
- Book by two authors: list both authors in MLA order.
- Three authors: list all authors according to MLA seventh edition examples.
- More than three authors: use the first author followed by `et al.`.
- Edited book: include editor role as required by MLA.
- Journal article: include article title, journal title, volume/issue/date/pages, medium.
- Translation: include translator information.
- Dissertation or thesis: identify dissertation/thesis type, institution, year, medium.
- Web resource: include publication or copyright date when available and access date.

## Chinese Reference Patterns

- Chinese entries follow the local examples in the template and appear after English references.
- Use Chinese punctuation in Chinese references.
- For Chinese authors, use Chinese comma `，` after the author name.
- For Chinese edited collections, use the Chinese dash convention shown in the template, such as `——`.
- Keep Chinese book, journal, and collection examples structurally consistent with the supplied reference template.

## Parenthetical Citations

- English MLA-style source by pinyin/English author name: `(Zhang 6)`.
- Chinese source by Chinese author: `(张中载 6)`.
- Chinese translation by a foreign author using a Chinese translated name: `(巴特 6)`.
- Chinese source with two Chinese authors: `(张某、李某 6)`.
- Chinese source with three or more Chinese authors: `(张某等 6)`.
- If Works Cited lists a Chinese author writing in English with pinyin author name, use strict MLA author-page format, e.g. `(Zhang 6)`.
- Keep author names in citations aligned with the corresponding Works Cited entry.

## Editing Rules

- Do not fabricate missing source metadata.
- Do not change an author's name format unless the Works Cited entry and all matching in-text citations are updated together.
- Do not mix Chinese and English punctuation inside the same citation style unless the template example requires it.
- Flag incomplete entries for user review instead of guessing publication place, publisher, date, URL, or access date.
